## One-Sentence Summary

Schmid et al. (2024) report the final overall-survival analysis of the phase 3 KEYNOTE-522 trial (N=1174): neoadjuvant pembrolizumab plus platinum-taxane-anthracycline chemotherapy followed by adjuvant pembrolizumab significantly improved 5-year OS (86.6% vs 81.7%; P=0.002), confirming durable benefit beyond the previously reported pCR and EFS gains — establishing the pembrolizumab-containing regimen as the reference immunotherapy context for early-stage TNBC, without supplying WES-level genomic biomarkers for response prediction.

---

## Key Contributions

- Presents the **seventh prespecified interim analysis** (data cutoff March 22, 2024; median follow-up 75.1 months) of KEYNOTE-522, the first phase 3 trial to show a **statistically significant overall-survival benefit** with neoadjuvant plus adjuvant anti-PD-1 therapy in early-stage TNBC.
- Confirms **5-year OS superiority** of pembrolizumab–chemotherapy over placebo–chemotherapy (86.6% vs 81.7%; absolute difference 4.9 percentage points; P=0.002), meeting the prespecified alpha-adjusted threshold (0.00503).
- Provides **updated descriptive EFS** at >6 years (5-year EFS 81.2% vs 72.2%; HR 0.65, 95% CI 0.51–0.83), with reduced distant recurrence (9.8% vs 14.4%) as the dominant first event.
- Reports **OS benefit by pCR status**, suggesting the survival gain may exceed that expected from pCR improvement alone — relevant to interpreting pCR as a surrogate endpoint.
- Documents the **complete neoadjuvant/adjuvant pembrolizumab regimen** (2:1 randomization, stratification factors, dosing) that defines the B3 therapeutic context for TNBC checkpoint inhibition.

## Core Idea / Mechanism / Argument

### Claimed vs demonstrated

**Claimed:** Neoadjuvant pembrolizumab combined with standard platinum-containing chemotherapy, followed by adjuvant pembrolizumab, produces a clinically meaningful and statistically significant improvement in overall survival versus chemotherapy alone in high-risk, early-stage TNBC — extending prior pCR and EFS wins into a hard long-term endpoint and supporting guideline adoption.

**Demonstrated:** In 1174 randomized patients with median 75.1-month follow-up, pembrolizumab–chemotherapy reduced deaths (115/784 [14.7%] vs 85/390 [21.8%]; P=0.002) and improved Kaplan–Meier 5-year OS by 4.9 percentage points. Updated EFS shows a 35% relative risk reduction (HR 0.65). The trial was not designed to isolate neoadjuvant versus adjuvant pembrolizumab contributions, and biomarker analyses remain ongoing.

### Information flow

1. **Population:** Centrally confirmed early-stage TNBC (stage II–III; T1c N1–2 or T2–4 N0–2), ECOG 0–1, no prior systemic therapy; PD-L1 status not required for enrollment.
2. **Randomization (2:1):** Pembrolizumab–chemotherapy (784) vs placebo–chemotherapy (390), stratified by nodal status, tumor size (T1–T2 vs T3–T4), and carboplatin schedule (weekly vs q3w).
3. **Neoadjuvant phase (~24 weeks):** Four 3-week cycles of pembrolizumab 200 mg (or placebo) + paclitaxel 80 mg/m² weekly + carboplatin (AUC 5 q3w or AUC 1.5 weekly), then four 3-week cycles of pembrolizumab/placebo + doxorubicin 60 mg/m² (or epirubicin 90 mg/m²) + cyclophosphamide 600 mg/m².
4. **Surgery:** Definitive surgery 3–6 weeks after last neoadjuvant cycle; pCR assessed as ypT0–Tis ypN0.
5. **Adjuvant phase:** Radiation as indicated + pembrolizumab or placebo q3w for up to nine cycles; adjuvant capecitabine not permitted.
6. **Endpoints:** Co-primary pCR and EFS (reported previously); key secondary OS (analyzed here with alpha passed from prior primary-endpoint significance).

### Novel vs inherited

- **Inherited:** Phase 3 double-blind design, regimen, and primary pCR (+13.6 percentage points; P<0.001) and EFS (HR 0.63; P<0.001 at ~39 months) results from earlier interim analyses (refs 10, 11).
- **Novel in this report:** Final OS analysis at ~6.3-year median follow-up; updated EFS and distant-recurrence rates; OS subgroup analyses including pCR stratification; piecewise OS hazard modeling showing delayed separation of curves after 2 years (weighted average HR for death 0.66).

## Methodology / Evidence Base

### Design
Phase 3, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled, multinational clinical trial (181 sites + 2 satellites, 21 countries). Intention-to-treat efficacy analyses; as-treated safety population (783 pembro, 389 placebo received ≥1 drug or surgery).

### Sample / setting
- **N randomized:** 1174 (784 pembrolizumab–chemotherapy; 390 placebo–chemotherapy).
- **Enrollment:** March 2017 – September 2018.
- **Data cutoff:** March 22, 2024 (seventh prespecified interim analysis, ~84 months after first randomization).
- **Median follow-up:** 75.1 months (range 65.9–84.0).
- **Deaths at cutoff:** 200 total (67.3% information fraction); 115 pembro vs 85 placebo.

### Intervention or exposure
- **Experimental:** Neoadjuvant pembrolizumab 200 mg IV q3w × 4 + paclitaxel/carboplatin × 12 weeks, then pembrolizumab/placebo q3w × 4 + anthracycline–cyclophosphamide × 12 weeks → surgery → adjuvant pembrolizumab 200 mg q3w up to 9 cycles.
- **Control:** Identical chemotherapy backbone with placebo replacing pembrolizumab in both neoadjuvant and adjuvant phases.

### Measurements / instruments
- **pCR:** Pathological stage ypT0–Tis ypN0 at definitive surgery (co-primary; first interim).
- **EFS:** Time from randomization to progression precluding surgery, local/distant recurrence, second primary cancer, or death (co-primary; investigator-assessed, blinded).
- **OS:** Time from randomization to death from any cause (key secondary; stratified log-rank; stratified Cox HRs).
- **PD-L1:** Central IHC 22C3 pharmDx; combined positive score (CPS) — exploratory subgroup only.
- **Safety:** CTCAE v4.0; immune-mediated AEs by prespecified MedDRA terms.

### Statistical analysis approach
Kaplan–Meier estimates; stratified log-rank and Cox models using randomization stratification factors; Lan–DeMets O'Brien–Fleming alpha spending; OS tested at one-sided alpha 0.00503 after step-down from significant pCR and EFS; EFS in this report descriptive only (significance established at fourth interim). Trial powered 82% to detect OS HR 0.70 at one-sided alpha 0.025.

### Replication or pre-registration status
Registered NCT03036488; protocol and SAP prespecified; sponsor-funded (Merck); independent data monitoring committee oversight.

## Assumptions

- Early-stage high-risk TNBC (T1c N1–2 or T2–4 N0–2) represents the intended clinical population for extrapolating biomarker findings.
- The 2:1 randomization and ITT analysis adequately estimate population-level treatment effects.
- pCR (ypT0–Tis ypN0) remains a valid — though imperfect — surrogate for long-term benefit in this setting.
- Prior interim pCR (+13.6 pp) and EFS (HR 0.63) results remain valid at the time of this OS report.
- PD-L1 CPS subgroup results are exploratory and not definitive for biomarker claims.
- Chemotherapy backbone (weekly or q3w carboplatin + standard agents) reflects contemporary high-intensity TNBC NACT.

---

## Limitations / Failure Modes

- **No genomic biomarker data** in this publication; cannot directly answer AQ-1 or rank WES features.
- Trial **not designed to separate neoadjuvant vs adjuvant pembrolizumab** contributions to OS/EFS.
- **Adjuvant capecitabine prohibited** — residual-disease management differs from post-CREATE-X practice.
- **EFS descriptive only** at this analysis (significance declared at earlier interim); updated HR 0.65 is not a new formal hypothesis test.
- **OS curves cross early** (piecewise HR 0.87 before 2 years, 0.51 after); average treatment effect is delayed.
- **Subgroup analyses underpowered**; small subgroups (e.g., CPS <1, age ≥65) may reflect random variation.
- **Immune-mediated AEs** more frequent with pembrolizumab (35.0% any grade vs 13.1%), including endocrinopathies that may require long-term hormone replacement.

## Key Quotes

> "We randomly assigned, in a 2:1 ratio, patients with previously untreated stage II or III triple-negative breast cancer to receive neoadjuvant therapy with four cycles of pembrolizumab (at a dose of 200 mg) or placebo every 3 weeks plus paclitaxel and carboplatin, followed by four cycles of pembrolizumab or placebo plus doxorubicin–cyclophosphamide or epirubicin–cyclophosphamide."

> "The estimated overall survival at 60 months was 86.6% (95% confidence interval [CI], 84.0 to 88.8) in the pembrolizumab–chemotherapy group, as compared with 81.7% (95% CI, 77.5 to 85.2) in the placebo–chemotherapy group (P = 0.002)."

> "At the first interim analysis, the addition of pembrolizumab to platinum-containing neoadjuvant chemotherapy resulted in a significant increase in pathological complete response, defined as pathological stage ypT0–Tis ypN0 … of 13.6 percentage points (P<0.001)."

> "A total of 159 patients (20.3%) in the pembrolizumab–chemotherapy group and 114 patients (29.2%) in the placebo–chemotherapy group had an event or died (hazard ratio, 0.65; 95% CI, 0.51 to 0.83)."

> "The estimated 5-year event-free survival was 81.2% (95% CI, 78.3 to 83.8) in the pembrolizumab–chemotherapy group and 72.2% (95% CI, 67.4 to 76.4) in the placebo–chemotherapy group."

> "The most common first event in the analysis of event-free survival in both groups was distant recurrence, with 77 events (9.8%) in the pembrolizumab–chemotherapy group and 56 events (14.4%) in the placebo–chemotherapy group."

> "Although the results of this nonrandomized comparison warrant cautious interpretation, they suggest that the overall survival benefit with pembrolizumab may exceed that expected from the improvement in pathological complete response alone."

> "The trial was not designed to distinguish the relative efficacy contributions in the long-term outcomes from the individual treatment phases."

> "Analyses of molecular biomarkers that might predict clinical response to pembrolizumab are ongoing."

> "The benefit with respect to overall survival was observed in certain patient subgroups defined according to prognostic risk factors, including lymph-node involvement and tumor size, and was observed regardless of PD-L1 expression status and the outcome with respect to pathological complete response."
